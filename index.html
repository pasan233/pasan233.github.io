An Island Like No Other

Sri Lanka, historically called Serendib and Ceylon, rests peacefully in the blue waters of the Indian Ocean. Its history goes back thousands of years, boasting deep roots in Buddhist traditions and a legendary royal ancestry that created vast ancient cities.

Today, the nation is an incredible crossroads of diverse natural biomes. Travel from sun-drenched coastal beaches up to misty mountain tea plantations in just a few short hours. The unique hospitality of its people welcomes everyone with warm smiles.

Top Historical & Natural Destinations

Take a look at the spectacular variety of regions across the island:

Sigiriya Lion Rock Fortress

Rising nearly 200 meters high, this towering rock stronghold was built by King Kashyapa in the 5th century. It features beautiful ancient frescoes, a massive stone lion gateway, and gorgeous sky gardens at its summit.

The Historic Galle Fort

A beautiful coastal fortress built by European colonial empires on the southwest coast. Walk along old stone ramparts, pass majestic lighthouses, and explore streets lined with boutique cafes and vibrant art displays.

The Sacred City of Kandy

Nestled safely among the green hills, Kandy is the final capital of the ancient kings. It proudly hosts the Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic, one of Buddhism's holiest shrines.

Mirissa & Southern Beaches

A classic destination for pristine golden beaches, palm trees, and fantastic surf. It is also famous as one of the best locations in the world for catching a glimpse of giant blue whales out in the ocean.

Rich Heritage & Tradition

The spirit of Sri Lanka is found within its living arts. Traditional dances feature lively, acrobatic movements, dramatic rhythms on Kandyan drums, and beautifully carved wooden masks representing folklore stories.

Food is another major pillar of life here. Meals center around rice served with various rich curries filled with native spices, paired with crispy "hoppers" (savory rice-flour crepes) and fiery sambols.
